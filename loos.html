Adolf Loos – Ornament and Crime (1908)

In 1908, architect Adolf Loos published his essay "Ornament and Crime," arguing that ornament was culturally regressive and economically wasteful.

Adolf Loos, Looshaus, Vienna, 1910

Loos believed that modern society should reject decorative excess in favor of clean, undecorated surfaces. His ideas strongly influenced modernist architecture and design.

This moment marks a dramatic ideological shift: ornament, once considered the soul of design, was now framed as a burden. The debate between decoration and function would define much of 20th-century design history.
